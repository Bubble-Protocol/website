Protecting sensitive data on a decentralised web
Bubble Protocol enables blockchains to control and govern private data.

Your data, your rules.

Over the past three decades the centralized structure of the web has significantly compromised our privacy. Cyberattacks, data trading, and data harvesting all pose serious threats to our individual autonomy. Web 3.0 has a vision to transform the underlying architecture of the web, decentralising services and returning control of personal data to users. It’s goal is simple: your data, your rules. Towards this goal, Bubble Protocol is developing critical infrastructure to integrate private user data into decentralised applications in a way that is both secure and simple to use, while unlocking a multitude of innovation possibilities.
Bubble Protocol creates a bridge between Web3 and private data. Bubble Protocol is an open-source software protocol that lets blockchains control and govern private data. It allows on-chain smart contracts to directly control access to off-chain data, whether held on private cloud servers, decentralised storage networks or private company databases.

Key Features

Secure Off-chain Storage Private data is held in a protective ‘bubble’ on a secure relay server of the user’s choosing.
On-Chain Access Controls Each bubble is protected by a smart contract, which defines who can access what data under what conditions and acts as a service level agreement.
Fully Customisable A bubble smart contract is fully customisable by the developer, enabling a wide range of use cases and applications.

How It Works

Bubble protocol is designed for developers of decentralised applications and companies looking to move into Web3.
Step 1 Design a smart contract to govern your data. *
Step 2 Deploy your smart contract to the blockchain of your choice.
Step 3 Create your bubble on a relay server of your choice. †
Step 4 Store your data off-chain under the protection of your bubble.
Step 5 Interact with your bubble’s data and smart contract.

* A Bubble smart contract is a state machine tailored to control the life cycle of the data for the application you are building. Specify who can access the data and under what conditions. Specify smart contract transitions to manage the data life cycle. Use NFT contracts or on-chain oracles to control access to your data based on external events. The possibilities are endless.

† Use a cloud relay service like the Bubble Private cloud, install Bubble Protocol on your home or company server, or integrate Bubble Protocol with your existing database or infrastructure.

Use Cases

Developers
Decentralised Applications Create a bubble for each user to store their personal data and app settings. Use it to sync data across devices. Read and write to your bubbles easily via a restful API confident that your smart contract will authenticate your users.
NFTs Store restricted content in a bubble accessible only to NFT owners. Differentiate between NFT-specific private content, NFT-wide private content and public content in the same bubble.
Social Media Create a bubble to share private social media data between friends and use it to relay posts and messages. Let users choose their relay server and give them control over their own social media data.
Companies
Customer Accounts Create a bubble for each customer to hold their personal data and account settings. Use the smart contract as their service level agreement and let the bubble manage privacy compliance and consent management for you. Allow different clients in a supply chain to read customer data at appropriate stages of the service lifecycle.
Paywall Provide access to private content on a pay-per-view or subscription basis. Take payment directly in your bubble’s smart contract.
Web3 Innovation Integrate Bubble Protocol into your existing data infrastructure and step lightly into Web3. Run a Web3 service in parallel with your current service using the same backend databases. Get a headstart in Web3 and experiment with the technology today.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Bubble Protocol? Bubble Protocol is an open source project designed to allow blockchains to control private data by using off-chain storage folders called "bubbles" on private servers and on-chain smart contracts for access control. It enables secure and customizable off-chain data management for decentralized applications, NFTs, and various services within the Web 3.0 ecosystem.
How does Bubble Protocol ensure data privacy? Bubble Protocol ensures data privacy through a combination of off-chain storage and on-chain access controls, which together create a secure environment for handling private data. Data is stored in "bubbles" on private servers chosen by the user or the dApp developer. This keeps sensitive data off the blockchain and off of public storage networks, reducing the risk of exposing private information. Data is encrypted by default so that only the parties to the contract can read the data.
How is Bubble Protocol decentralised? Bubble Protocol achieves decentralization primarily by allowing users to select their own relay servers and through the separation of concerns between data storage and access control. This separation allows Bubble relay servers to be very simple data storage nodes that can host any bubble for any purpose making them application agnostic. It means relay servers are like miniature DropBox servers with no knowledge of who owns the bubbles they host or what they are used for. Since all relay servers are capable of hosting any bubble it makes it easy for users to change relay services to seek the best service.
What are the advantages of using Bubble Protocol? Bubble Protocol makes it simple for DApp developers to integrate authenticated off-chain data storage into their applications and has the additional benefit of being secure enough for use with confidential data. For companies, the ability to integrate Bubble Protocol into existing server infrastructure makes it easy to join Web3 or experiment with Web3 technology.
How can I integrate Bubble Protocol into my project? See the TODO list example or download the Bubble Tools command line interface. For more information contact us by email or join our Discord server - we would be very interested to hear about your project and are always pleased to help.
Are there any limitations to Bubble Protocol? Bubble Protocol is compatible with any EVM-based blockchain but currently only supports the BASE Goerli testnet. By design, users and developers need to rely on private servers for data storage. This introduces a potential point of failure depending on the chosen server. We envisage a future where independent relay service providers combine to provide trusted networks of relays to eliminate single points of failure.
